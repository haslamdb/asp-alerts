Overview

The Surgical Prophylaxis module provides real-time monitoring and validation of surgical antibiotic prophylaxis following ASHP/IDSA/SHEA/SIS clinical practice guidelines. The module validates agent selection, timing, duration, and dosing for perioperative antibiotics.

Timing Guidelines

Antibiotic Class Recommended Timing Notes
Standard antibiotics
(cefazolin, ampicillin-sulbactam)		 Within 60 minutes of incision Optimal: 30-60 minutes before
Vancomycin Within 120 minutes of incision Requires slow infusion (1-2 hours)
Fluoroquinolones Within 120 minutes of incision Longer infusion time required

Duration Guidelines

Procedure Type Maximum Duration Notes
Most procedures ≤24 hours post-op Single dose often sufficient for clean procedures
Cardiac surgery ≤48 hours post-op Extended duration acceptable
Clean procedures Single dose No post-op doses needed

Key Principle: Prophylaxis should be discontinued within 24 hours for most procedures. Extended duration does not reduce SSI risk and increases resistance.

Re-dosing During Surgery

For prolonged procedures, re-dosing is recommended based on antibiotic half-life:

Antibiotic Re-dose Interval From First Dose
Cefazolin Every 4 hours Re-dose if surgery >4 hours
Cefoxitin Every 2 hours Re-dose if surgery >2 hours
Ampicillin-sulbactam Every 2 hours Re-dose if surgery >2 hours
Vancomycin Not typically needed Long half-life

Also re-dose if significant blood loss (>1500 mL) occurs during surgery.

Weight-Based Dosing

Antibiotic Standard Dose Weight-Based Adjustment
Cefazolin 2g IV ≥120 kg: 3g IV
Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV Max 2g per dose
Gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV Use adjusted body weight if obese

Procedure-Specific Recommendations

Cardiac Surgery

  • Primary: Cefazolin 2g (3g if ≥120kg)
  • MRSA risk/colonized: Add vancomycin 15 mg/kg
  • Beta-lactam allergy: Vancomycin + gentamicin
  • Duration: ≤48 hours

Colorectal Surgery

  • Primary: Cefazolin 2g + Metronidazole 500mg
  • Alternative: Ertapenem 1g
  • Mechanical bowel prep: With oral antibiotics if used
  • Duration: ≤24 hours

Orthopedic Surgery (with implant)

  • Primary: Cefazolin 2g (3g if ≥120kg)
  • MRSA colonized: Add vancomycin
  • Beta-lactam allergy: Vancomycin or clindamycin
  • Duration: ≤24 hours

Neurosurgery

  • Craniotomy: Cefazolin 2g
  • CSF shunt: Cefazolin 2g (some add vancomycin)
  • Spinal with hardware: Cefazolin 2g
  • Duration: ≤24 hours

MRSA Considerations

Add vancomycin to standard prophylaxis when:

  • Known MRSA colonization (positive nasal screen)
  • Recent MRSA infection
  • High institutional MRSA rates
  • Cardiac surgery with sternotomy
  • Orthopedic surgery with implant (per institutional protocol)

Note: Vancomycin should be added to (not replace) cefazolin for gram-negative coverage.

Allergy Alternatives

Allergy Type Can Use Avoid
Penicillin (non-severe) Cefazolin (low cross-reactivity) Ampicillin, piperacillin
Penicillin (severe/anaphylaxis) Vancomycin, clindamycin All beta-lactams
Cephalosporin Vancomycin, clindamycin All cephalosporins

Compliance Metrics

The module tracks the following quality metrics:

  • Timing compliance: % of cases with prophylaxis within recommended window
  • Agent compliance: % of cases with guideline-concordant antibiotic
  • Duration compliance: % of cases discontinued within recommended timeframe
  • Dosing compliance: % of cases with weight-appropriate dosing
  • Overall compliance: % of cases meeting all criteria
